DefinitionAn overdraft allows a business to temporarily spend more money than it has in its bank account, up to an agreed limit.
Used forVery short-term needs, usually a few days or a few weeks.
ExampleThe business has no money in its account, but the overdraft lets it go negative to pay wages to staff.
Advantages
  • Quick and flexible, only used when needed.
Disadvantages
  • The interest rate is usually high.
  • The bank can cancel the limit at short notice.
DefinitionThe selling of accounts receivable for a discounted price to a factoring company.
Accounts receivableMoney your customers owe you for goods or services they have not paid for yet.
VideoA short, visual explainer covering what factoring is, how it works step by step, and why it matters. Captions and transcript included. Watch on YouTube ↗
Advantages
  • Improves cash flow quickly.
Disadvantages
  • Receives less than the full invoice value.
  • Heavy reliance can signal cash-flow problems.
DefinitionA loan secured by the property of the borrower or business.
SecuredThe lender has something valuable to rely on if the borrower cannot repay. This is something the business already owns, for example a building or land.
Used forLarge, long-term purchases such as land or property, repaid over many years, with regular payments that cover both the loan and the interest.
Advantages
  • Spreads a large cost over a long period.
  • Lower interest rates than most short-term debt.
Disadvantages
  • The property can be lost if repayments are not met.
  • If the interest rate goes up, the repayments go up too.
DefinitionA long-term source of debt finance where a business pays to use an asset for an agreed period of time instead of buying it outright.
Why lease?Some assets are very expensive to buy outright, for example machinery or a building.
ExampleA car-hire business leases its cars instead of buying them, making regular lease payments.
Advantages
  • No large payment upfront.
  • Can upgrade to newer equipment whenever the lease ends.
  • The business does not carry the risk of the asset losing value over time.
Disadvantages
  • The business never owns the asset.
DefinitionProfits kept inside the business after expenses, tax and any dividends have been paid.
Key ideaA business can choose how much profit to keep and reinvest, so it is flexible.
ExampleA business keeps part of last year's profit and uses it to open a second store, rather than borrowing the money.
Advantages
  • No interest and no repayments.
  • No loss of ownership or control.
Disadvantages
  • Only available if the business is profitable.
DefinitionShares are small parts of ownership in a company. A company can raise finance by selling shares to investors.
OwnershipOwning part of the company, which can include a say in decisions and a share of the profits.
ImportantSole traders and partnerships cannot issue shares. Only companies can:
  • Private companies (Pty Ltd) can have shares, but cannot sell them to the general public.
  • Public companies can sell shares to the public, for example on the ASX (the Australian stock exchange).
Advantages
  • No repayment and no interest.
  • Can raise large amounts for growth.
Disadvantages
  • Ownership and control are shared with others.
  • Dividends may have to be paid to shareholders out of profits.

Practise

A few questions to practise with. If one trips you up, you will get a hint and can try again.

Question 1

Equity finance is money that:

Correct. Equity is money the business owns, with no repayment.

Question 2

Which source of finance is used for very short-term needs, usually a few days or weeks?

Correct. An overdraft covers very short-term cash needs of days or weeks.

Question 3

A medical business wants to use an expensive piece of medical equipment but cannot afford to buy it outright. Which source fits best?

Correct. Leasing lets the business use the equipment through regular payments, without buying it.

Question 4

A business is owed $10,000 by a customer in 60 days but needs cash now. Which source fits best?

Correct. Factoring turns unpaid invoices into cash straight away.

Question 5

A sole trader wants to raise finance. Which option is NOT available to them?

Correct. Only companies can issue shares, so a sole trader cannot.

Question 6

A business wants to fund long-term growth without taking on repayments or interest. Which source fits best?

Correct. Retained profits use the business's own money, with no interest or repayments.
